# 📝 Editorial Standards

> **Defines the writing, formatting, and editorial rules used when creating and maintaining Anu Sabi content.**

---

# Purpose

Editorial standards ensure that all game content maintains a consistent level of quality, readability, and professionalism.

These standards apply regardless of whether content is created by:

- Human writers
- Designers
- AI-assisted tools
- Rambler Engine generation
- Future community contributors

Consistency improves player trust and overall gameplay quality.

---

# Editorial Principles

Every phrase should be:

- Accurate
- Clear
- Readable
- Consistent
- Respectful
- Timeless whenever possible

Editorial quality should never be sacrificed for quantity.

---

# Spelling

Use correct spelling for every supported language.

Requirements:

- Verify official spellings.
- Avoid intentional misspellings.
- Avoid internet shorthand.
- Correct obvious typographical errors.

If multiple accepted spellings exist, use the officially recognized or most widely accepted version.

---

# Grammar

Grammar should follow the standard rules of the phrase's language.

Avoid:

- Incorrect verb forms
- Missing words
- Broken sentence structure
- Informal internet grammar

Common expressions may intentionally break grammar only if that is their accepted form.

---

# Capitalization

Use proper capitalization.

Examples:

Correct

- Harry Potter
- Chocolate Hills
- Manila Ocean Park

Incorrect

- harry potter
- CHOCOLATE HILLS
- manila ocean park

Consistency is more important than stylistic preference.

---

# Punctuation

Use punctuation only when it is part of the official phrase.

Examples:

Correct:

- Who Wants to Be a Millionaire?
- Spider-Man
- Dr. Jose Rizal

Avoid unnecessary punctuation that does not contribute to the official title.

---

# Numbers

Numbers should match the official form.

Examples:

- 7-Eleven
- Formula One
- World War II

Do not replace numbers with words unless that is the official version.

---

# Symbols

Avoid decorative symbols.

Examples to avoid:

- ★
- ❤
- ✓
- ™ (unless officially required)

Symbols should only appear when they are legitimately part of the phrase.

---

# Emoji

Emoji should never appear in phrase content.

Emoji may be used only in:

- Marketing
- Tutorials
- UI
- Notifications

Gameplay phrases should remain text-only.

---

# Abbreviations

Only use abbreviations that are widely recognized.

Examples:

Acceptable

- NBA
- NASA
- WHO

Avoid niche or organization-specific abbreviations that most players would not recognize.

---

# Proper Names

Verify the official spelling of:

- People
- Places
- Organizations
- Products
- Movies
- Songs
- Books

Proper names should always match their official presentation.

---

# Cultural Respect

Content should remain respectful across cultures.

Avoid:

- Offensive stereotypes
- Derogatory language
- Discriminatory expressions
- Insensitive wording

Anu Sabi is intended to be welcoming to a broad audience.

---

# Consistency

Maintain consistent formatting throughout the content library.

Examples:

If one movie title uses its official capitalization, all movie titles should follow the same approach.

Consistency is more important than personal writing preferences.

---

# Readability

Players should be able to quickly read the revealed answer.

Avoid:

- Excessive punctuation
- Decorative formatting
- Unnecessary capitalization
- Difficult-to-read formatting

Simple formatting improves gameplay.

---

# AI Editing

AI tools may assist with:

- Grammar correction
- Spelling verification
- Metadata suggestions
- Formatting validation

AI should not make final editorial decisions without human review.

---

# Human Review

Every approved phrase should ideally receive editorial review.

Review includes checking:

- Spelling
- Grammar
- Formatting
- Category
- Difficulty
- Metadata
- Gameplay quality

Human approval remains the final authority.

---

# Editorial Checklist

Before approving a phrase, verify:

- Correct spelling
- Correct grammar
- Proper capitalization
- Appropriate punctuation
- Official wording
- Appropriate category
- Correct difficulty
- Consistent formatting
- Respectful language
- Complete metadata

---

# Future Improvements

Future editorial tooling may include:

- Automated spell checking
- AI grammar validation
- Duplicate detection
- Consistency reports
- Metadata validation
- Content linting

Automation should support editors, not replace them.
