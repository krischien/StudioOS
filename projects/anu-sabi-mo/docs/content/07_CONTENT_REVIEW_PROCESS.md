# 🔍 Content Review Process

> **Defines the official workflow for creating, reviewing, approving, releasing, updating, and retiring content within Anu Sabi.**

---

# Purpose

A structured review process ensures that every phrase released to players meets the quality standards of Anu Sabi.

The review process helps maintain:

- Accuracy
- Consistency
- Fair gameplay
- Editorial quality
- Long-term maintainability

Every piece of content should pass through this workflow before release.

---

# Design Principles

The review process should be:

- Simple
- Repeatable
- Transparent
- Scalable
- Collaborative

The workflow should remain effective regardless of team size.

---

# Content Lifecycle

Every phrase follows the same lifecycle.

```
Idea

↓

Draft

↓

Editorial Review

↓

Gameplay Review

↓

Approval

↓

Release

↓

Monitoring

↓

Revision (if needed)

↓

Archive
```

Each stage has a specific purpose and quality checkpoint.

---

# Stage 1: Idea

A new phrase begins as a concept.

Sources may include:

- Human writers
- Research
- AI-assisted generation
- Rambler Engine generation
- Community suggestions
- Educational content

Ideas should not be released directly into the game.

---

# Stage 2: Draft

The phrase is created along with its initial metadata.

Typical tasks include:

- Writing the phrase
- Assigning a category
- Estimating difficulty
- Adding tags
- Selecting language
- Completing required metadata

At this stage, the content is considered incomplete.

---

# Stage 3: Editorial Review

Editorial review verifies writing quality.

Review includes:

- Spelling
- Grammar
- Capitalization
- Punctuation
- Formatting
- Official naming
- Consistency

Editorial review focuses on presentation rather than gameplay.

---

# Stage 4: Gameplay Review

Gameplay review evaluates how the phrase performs during play.

Review includes:

- Difficulty assessment
- Recognition fairness
- Gibberish quality
- Timer suitability
- Hint usefulness
- Category fit

The goal is an enjoyable player experience.

---

# Stage 5: Approval

Once all checks pass, the phrase is approved.

Approval confirms that:

- Editorial standards are met.
- Gameplay standards are met.
- Metadata is complete.
- The phrase is ready for release.

Only approved content should enter production.

---

# Stage 6: Release

Approved phrases are included in an official content release.

Release methods may include:

- Application updates
- Content packs
- Seasonal events
- Downloadable content
- Cloud synchronization

The release mechanism should not affect the review workflow.

---

# Stage 7: Monitoring

After release, content should be monitored using gameplay data.

Metrics may include:

- Solve rates
- Hint usage
- Skip frequency
- Average completion time
- Player feedback
- Error reports

Monitoring helps identify content that requires adjustment.

---

# Stage 8: Revision

Content may be revised when necessary.

Reasons include:

- Editorial corrections
- Difficulty adjustments
- Metadata improvements
- Localization updates
- Gameplay balancing

Every revision should preserve version history.

---

# Stage 9: Archive

Content may eventually be archived.

Reasons include:

- Outdated references
- Licensing changes
- Quality issues
- Seasonal expiration
- Replacement by improved content

Archived content remains available for historical reference but is no longer distributed.

---

# Review Roles

Future workflows may include dedicated responsibilities.

Possible roles:

- Content Creator
- Editor
- Gameplay Reviewer
- Localization Reviewer
- AI Assistant
- Release Manager

A single individual may perform multiple roles in smaller teams.

---

# AI-Assisted Review

AI tools may assist by:

- Detecting spelling errors
- Suggesting metadata
- Estimating difficulty
- Finding duplicate phrases
- Identifying inconsistencies

AI recommendations should always be reviewed by a human before approval.

---

# Rambler Engine Integration

Content generated by the Rambler Engine should follow the same review process.

Suggested workflow:

```
Generate Phrase

↓

Validate Structure

↓

Estimate Metadata

↓

Editorial Review

↓

Gameplay Review

↓

Approval
```

Procedural generation does not bypass quality assurance.

---

# Review Checklist

Before approval, verify:

- Correct spelling
- Correct grammar
- Accurate metadata
- Appropriate category
- Appropriate difficulty
- Fair gameplay
- Consistent formatting
- No duplicate content
- Respectful language
- Complete review notes

---

# Continuous Improvement

The review process should evolve over time.

Future improvements may include:

- Automated validation
- AI-assisted review
- Analytics-driven recommendations
- Collaborative review tools
- Content quality dashboards

Continuous improvement strengthens the overall content library.
