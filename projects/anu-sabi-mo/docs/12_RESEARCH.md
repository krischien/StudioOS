# 🔬 Research & Validation

> *Good ideas inspire games. Great research builds great games.*

---

# Purpose

Research ensures that every significant decision made during the development of Anu Sabi is informed by evidence, testing, player feedback, or thoughtful analysis rather than assumptions alone.

This document defines the principles, workflows, and standards for researching new ideas throughout the lifetime of the project.

Detailed research artifacts are maintained within the project's `research/` directory.

---

# Research Philosophy

Research exists to reduce uncertainty.

Every major decision should answer at least one of the following questions:

- Does this improve gameplay?
- Does this support the product vision?
- Does this solve a real player problem?
- Is this technically feasible?
- Is this sustainable over time?

# Research Sources

Research should draw from multiple sources when appropriate.

Examples include:

- Player feedback
- Internal playtesting
- Analytics
- Academic publications
- Industry best practices
- Community discussions
- Competitor analysis

No single source should determine a major product decision.

---

# Validation Process

Every significant proposal should follow a consistent workflow.

```text
Idea
    ↓
Initial Research
    ↓
Discussion
    ↓
Prototype (if needed)
    ↓
Internal Review
    ↓
Playtesting
    ↓
Decision
    ↓
Documentation
    ↓
Implementation
```

---

# Player Feedback

Player feedback is one of the most valuable research inputs.

Feedback may be collected through:

- Beta testing
- Surveys
- Reviews
- Community discussions
- Issue reports
- Feature requests

Feedback should be categorized and reviewed regularly.

---

# Competitor Research

Competitor research exists to understand the market, not to copy it.

Areas of comparison include:

- Gameplay systems
- User experience
- Monetization
- Accessibility
- Content organization
- Retention strategies

The goal is to identify opportunities for innovation.

---

# Experimentation

Some ideas require experimentation before approval.

Possible experiments include:

- Prototype interfaces
- Gameplay variations
- Reward adjustments
- New game modes
- Content formats

Experiments should have clear success criteria before they begin.

---

# Decision Documentation

Every significant decision should be recorded.

Documentation should include:

- Problem statement
- Alternatives considered
- Decision made
- Reasoning
- Expected outcome
- Date
- Owner

Decision records are maintained in the project's `decisions/` directory.

---

# Research Repository

Detailed research documents are stored within:

```text
research/
```

Examples include:

- Competitor Analysis
- Market Research
- Player Interviews
- Academic References
- AI Research
- Feature Studies

This document serves as the policy, while the repository stores the supporting evidence.

---

# AI-Assisted Research

Artificial intelligence may assist with:

- Literature summaries
- Competitor comparisons
- Brainstorming
- Data organization
- Draft documentation

AI-generated findings should always be reviewed by a human before influencing product decisions.

---

# Success Metrics

The research process is successful when:

- Major decisions are evidence-based.
- Assumptions are validated early.
- Player feedback influences development.
- Documentation remains organized.
- The project avoids unnecessary rework.
