# ✍️ Phrase Guidelines

> **Defines the standards for creating high-quality phrases used throughout Anu Sabi.**

---

# Purpose

Phrases are the core gameplay content of Anu Sabi.

Every phrase should provide an enjoyable challenge while remaining fair, understandable, and appropriate for the target audience.

These guidelines ensure consistency regardless of whether a phrase is created by:

- A content writer
- A game designer
- AI-assisted tools
- The Rambler Engine
- Community contributors (future)

---

# Design Philosophy

A good Anu Sabi phrase should be:

- Familiar
- Recognizable
- Fun to decode
- Fair to solve
- Memorable

Players should feel rewarded when they correctly identify a phrase.

---

# Core Principles

Every phrase should aim to be:

- Clear
- Readable
- Appropriate
- Grammatically correct
- Culturally respectful
- Consistent with the game's tone

Quality always takes precedence over quantity.

---

# Phrase Selection

Suitable phrases include:

- Common expressions
- Famous quotes
- Movie titles
- Song titles
- TV shows
- Historical references
- Popular sayings
- Everyday language
- Educational content
- Well-known personalities
- Places
- Food
- Sports
- Technology
- Science
- General knowledge

The phrase should be recognizable to the intended audience.

---

# Avoid

Avoid phrases that are:

- Extremely obscure
- Offensive
- Hate speech
- Politically inflammatory
- Sexually explicit
- Graphic or violent
- Defamatory
- Copyright-sensitive where reproduction is inappropriate
- Dependent on temporary internet trends
- Excessively long
- Ambiguous without context

Content should remain suitable for a broad audience.

---

# Phrase Length

There is no fixed character limit.

However:

Short phrases

- Faster gameplay
- Easier recognition

Medium phrases

- Standard gameplay

Long phrases

- Higher difficulty
- Greater challenge
- Should remain readable

Length alone should not determine difficulty.

---

# Readability

Players should be able to read the original phrase comfortably once revealed.

Avoid:

- Excessive punctuation
- Random capitalization
- Emoji
- Decorative symbols
- Multiple abbreviations

Formatting should improve readability, not reduce it.

---

# Difficulty

Difficulty should come from recognition rather than confusion.

Factors influencing difficulty include:

- Phrase familiarity
- Word count
- Vocabulary
- Cultural specificity
- Similar sounding words
- Gibberish complexity

Difficulty assignments are defined separately in:

`05_DIFFICULTY_GUIDELINES.md`

---

# Categories

Every phrase should belong to at least one category.

Examples:

- Pinoy
- World
- Movies
- Music
- Food
- Sports
- Technology
- Animals
- Travel
- Education

Future versions may support multiple categories per phrase.

---

# Localization

Whenever possible:

- Avoid language-specific wordplay unless intended.
- Consider future translation requirements.
- Preserve the original meaning across languages.
- Allow culturally adapted equivalents where appropriate.

Localization standards are documented separately.

---

# Metadata

Each phrase should eventually include metadata such as:

- Unique ID
- Category
- Difficulty
- Language
- Tags
- Version
- Status
- Source
- Release pack

Metadata enables filtering, analytics, localization, and future content management.

---

# AI Compatibility

Phrases should be structured so they can be:

- Generated by AI
- Reviewed by humans
- Validated automatically
- Classified consistently

This allows future AI-assisted workflows while maintaining quality.

---

# Rambler Engine Compatibility

Future Rambler Engine content should follow these same standards.

Procedurally generated phrases should be:

- Readable
- Solvable
- Balanced
- Reviewed when necessary

The generation method must not reduce overall quality.

---

# Quality Checklist

Before approval, every phrase should satisfy the following checklist.

## Recognition

- Familiar to the intended audience
- Reasonably guessable

## Writing

- Correct spelling
- Correct grammar
- Consistent formatting

## Gameplay

- Appropriate difficulty
- Fair challenge
- No unintended ambiguity

## Technical

- Correct metadata
- Assigned category
- Assigned difficulty
- Version recorded

---

# Examples

## Good Examples

- Bahay Kubo
- Manila Ocean Park
- Harry Potter
- We Will Rock You
- Jurassic Park
- Chocolate Hills
- Adobo
- The Lion King

These are recognizable, readable, and suitable for gameplay.

---

## Poor Examples

- Extremely obscure internet memes
- Random keyboard text
- Internal company jokes
- Temporary viral hashtags
- Excessively long legal titles
- Offensive phrases
- Slang understood only by a very small audience

These reduce gameplay quality and should generally be avoided.
